POEMS BY ME

A dreamer's Reality

Whether it's crispy dose
Or trails in a jungle way
Eyes open early
At sharp six thirty
But only on Sunday

A sparkle of hope

Gazing at the endless
Dark night sky
That surrounds me
Like a thick blanket

A blanket made of velvet
Black velvet studded
With stars that sparkle
Brightly in your eyes

Breaking Free

Have you ever seen a
butterfly stuck inside glass
How it tries and tries to
Fly away into the cheerful

Sunshine, to feel its warm
Rays strike its wings
But getting struck by cold
glass instead and pushed back
into a realm of biting cold darkness

Golden Red and Brown

Soft breeze
rushes through the trees
wafting crispy leaves
to the blanket beneath my feet

Little green leaves
At its tips growing
Feel it coming
And welcoming

Dark green leaves
Right at the middle
afraid of falling
and withering

The beardy old ones
Of golden red tones
Smiling at me
wanting to be free

Let me go
And you will live
Thus saying
The rusty old leaves
Let go and fall asleep

Rainy day vs me

When the heavens
Break up and pour
Down with lightning flashing
And thunder booming

And chilly wind hits
You shiver and say
"Freezing cold day"

Roads become rivers
And you walk on
rushing water with
rubber chappals and
A grey umbrella

When hot soup and
hot snacks are devoured
with great relish
watching heavy rains through
frosted glass

I'm sitting here
Reading, solving linear algebra!

Tale of a tree

Have you ever spent
A quiet moment
listening to the stories
The rustle of the trees

We'd been together
Since forever
Holding our hands
Deep under the sands

Dancing under the moon
to the soft tune
of the cool breeze
Salty from the seas

Giving what we had
Sharing what we got
If they had something of us too
I would still be by you

That day had begun
With us lazing in the sun
That's when time came
With that dreadful pain

Only I could feel
her agony as cold steel
made it's way deep
dragging her to sleep

I still dance to the
notes of the breeze
And whisper to anyone
Who is willing to listen
The stories of my leaves

Ratri

Her purple hair is done
Just right
Her creamy skin in
The spotlight

My oily locks
In ugly tails
I grope in the dark
Camouflaged

She takes the stage
With a flattering smile
Charms them all with
Her cunning style

I watch her singing
Standing in the aisle
The thunderous cheer
Ringing in my ear

I take a step forward
In uncertain fear
Well there is no one to laugh
No one to jeer

With a racing heart
And a sinking spirit
I climb up the stage
With a raging calm
On my face

Deafening silence and
A hall fully empty
Am I too early? Am I too late?
For I see nobody around

Frosted Windows

Winter is the favourite
Season of my year
The season when you get
Fogged and frosted

The season when you
Get to reflect light
Warmth and joy
To rosy little brats

’Tis the festive season
When the aroma of cinnamon
And bread can bring the
Polar bears hibernating up north
To the delicious kitchen table

The season when warm
Faces press on you
Watching papa working on
The Christmas tree

The season when there is
Lively music and happy dance
A loving family and loving friends
And a beautiful present waiting to be opened

This winter is harsh
And cold proving
To me that
Ice can burn

My frosted glass
Is now frosted
Not with the cold snow
But with warm tears

Scorching tears
From the eyes of
The happy faces
Picturing papa working on
The Christmas tree

The Blindfold

When there’s nobody to ask
To even care if she smiles or cries
Her soul flutters, pining
For another blindfold over her eyes

To shut her eyes tightly
From the fierce rays of truth
She longs for a warm hug
Off the strong arms of a youth

He edges into her dreams
Stroking her hair softly, coming close
But she knows her vision's blurring
That love's a poppy and not a rose

She looks into troubled waters
With ripples her reflections clouded
But her image remained constant
Even when tranquility settled

To believe that she too
Will grow up to be a swan one day
And a prince Hans will take her
To a dreamland far away

To believe that the prince
Feeding the goat today
Wouldn't bait it tomorrow
As his hunting prey

No, She’s a lone lioness
With a fire burning inside
The queen of her own forest
Despondent emptiness beside

Cuz reality’s not the soft waves
That play tag with your tiny feet
It's the Tsunami that hits hard
Drowning every house every street

The world's full of crooks and
Liars and cold and the selfish
Love blinds, it's like the chocolate
A diabetic would relish

Well it'll always be winter
With people wrapping their eyes
With layers and layers of silk
Woven of false hopes and smiles

With warm smiles hiding
Cold intentions and hearts bare
Pouring brimming words, She longs for
Someone to love, someone to care

Reflections

A sweet little girl
Smiles back at me
Skipping stones on water
Hopping across the field

Amidst the yellow tulips
Singing merrily as she walks
With hands around dad's finger
Laughing as he talks

A nervous young girl
In uniform tie and shoes
Looks at me pleading
Desperate for an excuse

A blushing young maiden
Looks at me once more
Hoping he will notices
She leaves sighing, unsure

A tired young woman
Dark circles under her eyes
Give yourself a break I say
Quite late when we realize

A strong old woman
Having hiked many a peak
Sings and claps kissing
Softly her granddaughter's cheek

A wise old woman
Oh that's me!
Stares back from the mirror
Reflecting, smiles broadly
